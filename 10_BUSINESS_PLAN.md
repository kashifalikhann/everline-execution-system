## 2. THE OPPORTUNITY

### The Problem
Boring businesses (paving, roofing, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, janitorial) have a simple revenue model: get registered → find work → do work → get paid.

But the administrative overhead is crushing:
- 80% of sales time is paperwork, not selling
- Every government entity has a different registration process
- Bid portals are fragmented (BidNet, state portals, county websites, direct)
- Certifications expire and nobody tracks them
- Insurance requirements vary by entity
- Invoicing and payment collection takes 60-90 days

## 4. THE PRODUCT

### Phase 1: Registration Engine (Weeks 1-4)
**The wedge.** Get contractors registered with government entities.

Features:
- One-click registration to BidNet, state portals, county websites
- Document management (store BRC, PWCR, MBE, Insurance, W-9 once, use everywhere)
- AI-powered form filling (agents navigate portals, fill forms, submit)
- Registration status tracking across all entities
- Expiration alerts (certs, insurance, registrations)

### Phase 2: Operations Engine (Weeks 5-12)
**The sticky layer.** Once registered, manage everything.

Features:
- Bid monitoring (daily scan of all registered entity portals)
- Bid alert notifications (email/SMS when relevant RFP drops)
- Quote template generator (pre-fill scope, pricing, terms)
- Compliance calendar (track cert expirations, renewal deadlines)
- Insurance tracking (alert before policy expires)
- Entity relationship manager (who you're registered with, status, contacts)

### Phase 3: Revenue Engine (Weeks 13-24)
**The money layer.** Help them get paid.

Features:
- Invoice generation from completed work
- Payment tracking (government entities pay slow — track it)
- Accounts receivable aging
- Payment reminder automation
- Financial dashboard (revenue, outstanding, projected)
- Tax document management (1099s, sales tax, etc.)

### Phase 4: Intelligence Layer (Weeks 25-52)
**The moat.** Data-driven insights competitors can't replicate.

Features:
- Win/loss tracking (which bids do you win? Why?)
- Pricing intelligence (what are others bidding?)
- Entity spending analysis (who's buying what, when?)
- Optimal registration strategy (which entities give best ROI?)
- Market share tracking (how much of the market are you capturing?)

## 5. THE DATA ASSET (THE REAL BUSINESS)

### What We Build:
The complete US government entity database:
- **50 states**
- **3,143 counties** (and equivalents)
- **13,000+ school districts**
- **19,000+ municipalities**
- **35,000+ special districts** (water, sewer, fire, etc.)
- **Total: ~70,000+ entities**

### What We Track Per Entity:
- Registration process (exact steps, forms, required documents)
- Portal URLs and form fields
- Contact names, titles, emails, phones
- Bid notification patterns (when do they post RFPs?)
- Payment terms and average payment timeline
- Buying categories (what services do they buy?)
- Annual budget (where available)
- Cooperative memberships (BidNet, ESCNJ, Sourcewell, etc.)

### How We Build It:
1. **Direct download** — State DOE directories, municipal leagues, county associations (CSV/Excel)
2. **AI agent scraping** — Portal navigation, form field extraction, contact discovery
3. **Pakistan ops** — Manual verification, edge cases, quality control
4. **Service delivery learning** — Every registration teaches the system

### The Moat:
After 1,000 registrations: We know every entity's process better than anyone.
After 10,000 registrations: Unbreakable data moat. Competitors would need YEARS to replicate.
After 50,000 registrations: We ARE the government entity database for contractors.
