## SCRIPT 1: MUNICIPAL QPA (You reach the purchasing agent directly)

**Tone:** Professional, direct, confident. You're a qualified contractor asking to be listed — not begging.

---

**"Hi [QPA Name], my name is [Your Name], calling on behalf of Everline Coatings & Services. We're a New Jersey-based pavement maintenance contractor — parking lot striping, sealcoating, crack filling, pothole repairs, playground and athletic court markings.**

**We hold three state-level credentials: NJ Business Registration, Public Works Contractor Registration, and State-level MBE. We're also currently joining the Morris County Cooperative Pricing Council.**

**I'd like to get Everline added to [Municipality Name]'s approved vendor list. What's the process for that?"**

---

### They say: "We have an online form / BidNet"

**You reply:** "Great — I'll register right away. Is there anything else you need from us on our end? And what's the best email to send our qualification docs (BRC, PWCR, MBE, insurance, W-9) so they're on file?"

### They say: "Just send me an email"

**You reply:** "Perfect. What's your email address? And should I address it to you specifically, or is there a purchasing inbox? I'll send our full qualification package today."

### They say: "We already have contractors"

**You reply:** "I understand. I'm not asking to replace anyone — just to add us as a backup. We're fully credentialed with BRC, PWCR, and State MBE, we're local, and we can mobilize fast. Cost you nothing to have us on file. Can I send you our docs?"

### They say: "We don't have a vendor list"

**You reply:** "Got it. When you need pavement work done, who handles that — DPW? Facilities? What's the best way to make sure they know we're available and qualified?"

### They say: "Call back later / I'm busy"

**You reply:** "No problem — I'll send an email with everything. What's the best address? And when's a good time to follow up?"

### They say: "We use a cooperative"

**You reply:** "That's great — we're joining the Morris County Cooperative right now. Does that cover everything, or do you also keep a separate vendor list?"

## SCRIPT 2: MUNICIPAL CLERK BYPASS (Small boroughs with no QPA)

**Call the municipal main number.**

---

**"Hi, I'm [Your Name], calling from Everline Coatings & Services. We're a NJ pavement maintenance contractor trying to get set up as an approved vendor. Can you tell me who handles purchasing or vendor registration for [Borough Name]?"**

### They say: "That would be [Name/Title]"

**You reply:** "Great — are they available now? Or is there a direct number I can call?"

### They say: "We don't have anyone like that"

**You reply:** "No problem. Who handles public works or road maintenance? We're a pavement contractor — striping, sealcoating, crack filling, pothole repairs — and I want to make sure we're on file for when you need that work done."

**You reply:** "I understand. Which municipality do you use for shared services? And would you say it's okay if I reach out to them directly, or is there a process here?"

### They transfer you

**You reply:** "Thank you." [Wait for transfer, then use Script 1]

## CRITICAL RULES

1. **Never argue.** Acknowledge, redirect, ask for email.
2. **Always get an email address.** Even if they say no, ask "Where should I send this?"
3. **Send the email within 2 hours** of the call. The call primes them; the email delivers proof.
4. **Log before the next call.** Before you dial the next number, log the previous call.
5. **If they mention an upcoming project**, flag it as HIGH PRIORITY. Call back within 24 hours with pricing.
6. **Stand up while calling.** Your voice projects better. Sounds more confident.
7. **Smile while talking.** It literally changes how your voice sounds on the phone.

## WHAT TO SAY IF THEY ASK ABOUT PRICING

Don't give pricing on the phone. Say:

**"Happy to provide pricing once I understand what you need. Can I ask what type of pavement work you typically do — striping, sealcoating, crack filling? That way I send relevant numbers instead of generic ones."**

**Then:** Send pricing with the follow-up email. Attach it as a separate document alongside the 6 standard docs.

## WHAT TO SAY IF THEY ASK "HOW DID YOU GET THIS NUMBER"

**"We're doing outreach to all Morris County municipalities and school districts as part of joining the county cooperative. Your main office number was listed on the township website."**

Keep it simple. Don't overexplain.
